IMU Orientation Tracking & Panorama Reconstruction

Sensing & Estimation in Robotics, UC San Diego, Feb 2026
Project Report  /  Code
Python PyTorch Sensor Fusion Gradient Descent Quaternions / SO(3) Computer Vision

In this project, I developed a complete pipeline to recover accurate roll, pitch, and yaw angles from IMU measurements and construct high-quality panoramas. By formulating the orientation tracking as a gradient-descent optimization problem, the system effectively mitigates sensor noise and gyroscope drift.

Key Technical Implementations

  • Motion & Observation Models: Defined a motion model via gyroscope integration to estimate orientation trajectories. Created an observation model based on accelerometer data to align with the gravity direction, preventing long-term drift.
  • Trajectory Optimization (PyTorch): Formulated a cost function mapping relative rotation errors to a 3D rotation-vector (Lie algebra). Utilized torch.autograd for automatic differentiation to perform projected gradient descent on the unit-quaternion manifold.
  • Performance Validation: Compared optimized quaternion trajectories against Vicon motion-capture ground-truth data, achieving over 80% reduction in roll and pitch estimation errors.
  • Panorama Reconstruction: Transformed world-frame poses to the camera frame, mapped RGB image pixels to spherical coordinates, and projected them onto a cylindrical panorama to stitch continuous 2D environments.

Nonlinearity Modification of CMOS-MEMS Resonators

National Tsing Hua University / ICSS 2024
Project Paper
CMOS-MEMS TSMC 0.35 ¬µm Wet Etching Network Analyzer Nonlinear Dynamics FEM Simulation

This paper presents an experimental study addressing a critical performance bottleneck in miniaturized sensors: the limited power handling capabilities of MEMS resonators caused by amplitude-to-phase modulation (AM-PM) noise from nonlinear vibration.

Key Technical Achievements

  • Structural Design: Integrated small coupling springs at the ends of cantilever and fixed-fixed beam structures. These springs act as stress concentration features, storing more tension under high excitation to counter negative capacitive nonlinearity.
  • Microfabrication: Developed and released the devices utilizing the industry-standard TSMC 0.35 ¬µm CMOS process, followed by a precise sulfuric acid (H‚ÇÇSO‚ÇÑ) wet-etching process to remove sacrificial metals and suspend the micro-beam structures.
  • High-Frequency Measurement: Conducted precise S-parameter measurements using a 4-Port Network Analyzer inside a customized vacuum chamber to effectively eliminate air damping and squeeze-film effects.
  • Dynamic Tuning Results: Successfully proved that modifying cantilever beams with shorter springs mitigated softening nonlinearity (achieving a more linear response), while fixed-fixed beams exhibited enhanced hardening nonlinearity.
